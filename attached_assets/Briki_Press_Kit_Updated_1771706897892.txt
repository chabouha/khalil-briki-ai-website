PRESS KIT – Artistic Profile, Collaborations & Releases

EDUCATION

Briki’s musical journey began in Tunisia with early formation at the Ariana Public Conservatory, where foundational studies nurtured his technical skills and musical curiosity. He also benefited from mentorship within the Jazz Club de Tunis association, developing early improvisational skills and exposure to jazz traditions.

Originally a Northern African native who immigrated to Brazil in 2018, Briki brought a multicultural perspective and linguistic versatility to his studies. He is fluent in Arabic, Tunisian dialect, French, English, and Portuguese, which allows him to engage deeply with a broad spectrum of musical traditions.

At Bituca – Universidade de Música Popular, musical formation became more than academic study — it became immersion.

Guided by masters such as:

Enéías Xavier – one of the most respected figures in Brazilian instrumental music: a bassist, composer, arranger, and educator whose work includes translating the Comprehensive Bass Method by jazz legend Ron Carter into Portuguese, bringing this cornerstone of bass pedagogy into Brazil’s musical education. Enéías also performed alongside Milton Nascimento, connecting him directly to Brazil’s iconic popular music lineage. Ron Carter’s influence also resonates in the work of other jazz greats such as John Patitucci, situating Xavier within a lineage of global bass artistry.

Aloízio Horta – a prominent Brazilian bassist, composer, and educator rooted in instrumental music, jazz, and Afro-Brazilian rhythmic practices. His career includes award-winning compositions and performances that blend Brazilian popular music with improvisational sophistication.

Pitágoras Silveira – a pianist, composer, and educator who began his musical journey at Bituca in early adolescence, now active as a performer and teacher. His original work, including the solo album Imerso (2025), reflects deep engagement with composition, piano technique, and ensemble collaboration.

Through this immersion, Briki developed not only technical mastery but deep listening, musical dialogue, and compositional sensibility — building a musical voice informed by jazz, Gnawa rhythms, Arabic-Andalusian melodies, West African music, and Brazilian Popular Music (MPB), which together shaped his global, multicultural approach to performance and composition.

ARTISTIC PROFILE

At the center of diverse artistic intersections stands Khalil Briki, a Northern African native who immigrated to Brazil in 2018, bringing a multicultural, multilingual, and cross-continental perspective to his music. He is fluent in Arabic, Tunisian dialect, French, English, and Portuguese.

Briki plays bass, Gombri (Northern African acoustic bass), and oud. His music is informed by the rich traditions of jazz improvisation, Gnawa trance rhythms, Arabic-Andalusian melodic phrasing, West African polyrhythms, and Brazilian Popular Music (MPB), creating a hybridized sound that bridges continents, genres, and ancestral legacies. His immersion in Brazilian culture has further shaped his harmonic, rhythmic, and melodic sensibilities, allowing him to contribute authentically to Brazilian musical projects while integrating his global influences.

His presence is not merely supportive; it is architectural. Across concerts, festivals, and recordings, his harmonic sensibility and structural awareness help define the identity of each collaboration.

Throughout his trajectory, one principle remains constant: the balance between friendship and professionalism.

Many partnerships were born from personal connection — rehearsals that became conversations, exchanges that evolved into lasting artistic bonds. Yet it is precisely this trust, combined with discipline and rigor, that elevates each project beyond camaraderie into artistic excellence.

Briki’s path reveals a clear philosophy: Friendship fuels creativity. Professionalism sustains it.

The result is music built on mutual respect, shared vision, and collective growth.

SELECTED COLLABORATIONS
Khalil Briki Octet

As a personal project, the Khalil Briki Octet embodies Briki’s vision of Afro-descendant instrumental expression, blending ancestral rhythmic traditions with contemporary harmonic and melodic structures. The ensemble allows him to explore compositional ideas, improvisational interplay, and structural clarity within a larger group setting.

Formation:

Khalil Briki – composer and bass player

Lucas Godoy – drums

Dudu Amendoeira – piano and keys

Xande Sax – saxophone

Aurelio – trompete

Micael – trombone

Daniel Faria – guitar

Ebano Brandao – percussão

Through the Octet, Briki establishes a platform where Afro-Brazilian and global musical influences converge, emphasizing rhythmic dialogue, collective creativity, and harmonic architecture. The project highlights Briki’s role not only as a performer but as a composer and musical director, shaping the ensemble’s identity while honoring cultural roots.

With Igara Silva

Within Igara Silva’s evolving instrumental universe — deeply influenced by the tradition of Minas Gerais instrumental music and contemporary Brazilian composition — Briki’s bass became a grounding and structural force.

During creative and performance phases, the celebrated multi‑instrumentalist Paulo Santos, known for his work with the experimental ensemble Uakti and extensive contributions to Brazil’s avant‑garde and world music scene, also participated, adding sonic depth and textural richness to collaborative explorations. Marcus Ruffato additionally contributed in shows, enhancing live performances with his artistic presence.

Her musical language dialogues with the lyrical sophistication and harmonic depth associated with Minas Gerais’ rich instrumental legacy, while embracing modern textures and contemporary aesthetic approaches.

During the creative process of O Piano, os Cavalos e o Mar (2024), Briki contributed to arrangement development, helping shape a sound that honored this regional identity while expanding harmonic and textural possibilities.

Festival and award-stage performances reflect a trio dynamic built on listening, precision, and shared intention — where the melodic sensibility characteristic of Minas Gerais meets contemporary structural clarity, supported by Briki’s architectural bass work.

With Swing Safado

Within the Afro-descendant energy of Carnaval de Belo Horizonte, Swing Safado embodies the pulse of street bloco culture rooted in Black Brazilian rhythmic traditions.

Briki’s arrival strengthened rhythmic cohesion while honoring the Afro-Brazilian percussive foundations that define the band’s identity. His contribution introduced structural clarity without diluting the raw vitality of carnival expression.

The band features standout artists:

Dédé Santaklaus – a Latin Grammy-winning producer and multi-instrumentist, whose creative vision bridges traditional Afro-Brazilian rhythms with contemporary musical textures, strengthening the band’s sound architecture and groove.

Jeffim da Baze – a charismatic performer originating from the favelas, whose voice and energy channel the authentic spirit of community, resistance, and street culture. He is also a vocal advocate of anti-racist political engagement, embedding social awareness and cultural justice into Swing Safado’s music and performances.

Other contributors, such as Renegado and Adriana Araújo, add additional layers of rhythm, voice, and energy, supporting the ensemble without being central protagonists.

In 2026, Swing Safado toured Minas Gerais, culminating in performances on the emblematic Avenida dos Andradas, where more than 150,000 spectators experienced the band’s vibrant Afro-Brazilian rhythms and charismatic stage presence.

Through dialogue and shared cultural vision, Swing Safado evolved from vibrant street celebration into a project capable of navigating curated stages while preserving its Afro-diasporic roots, communal spirit, and socially conscious message.

With Thamires Cunha

In the show Raízes, Briki’s bass lines carried more than harmony — they carried memory.

Supporting Thamires Cunha’s Afro-descendant affirmation, her artistic voice — rooted in choro, samba, and Black Brazilian cultural memory — resonates with strength and historical consciousness. Briki’s playing establishes emotional depth and structural grounding for her clarinet and vocal expression.

Live performances reveal her melodies soaring over bass lines that anchor ancestry, resistance, and celebration — a collective statement shaped with sensitivity, respect, and artistic rigor.

With Silas Prado

Across instrumental jazz sessions and festival stages, Briki’s collaboration with Silas Prado unfolds through attentive interplay deeply informed by Afro-Brazilian rhythmic heritage.

Silas Prado’s musical language draws from jazz while remaining connected to Black diasporic traditions, and Briki’s bass engages this lineage with rhythmic awareness and harmonic sophistication.

Briki also participated in the presentation of Silas Prado’s album Obrigado Mestre, contributing to live interpretations that highlight both improvisational dialogue and the album’s compositional richness.

With Tatio

In groove-driven projects with Tatio, Briki demonstrates versatility beyond the bass, contributing guitar and expanding harmonic textures within contemporary Afro-Brazilian frameworks.

He participated in the artist’s first album presentation, Contrabandeado, helping shape the live realization of the recorded material and adding depth to both arrangement and performance.

The project gained further artistic dimension under the production of Chico Neves, one of Brazil’s most respected music producers.

Within this context, Briki’s role extends beyond performance: he contributes to structural decisions and harmonic architecture, reinforcing rhythmic matrices while preserving groove and identity.

Creative chemistry drives the collaboration, while professional rigor sustains it — experimentation unfolding within disciplined musical design.

With Gabriela Viegas

Collaborations with Gabriela Viegas explore territories influenced by African traditions and diasporic memory.

Rooted in rhythmic matrices echoing West and Central African heritage, her work bridges Brazilian popular music with ancestral narratives.

Briki’s bass interacts with percussion-driven arrangements and polyrhythmic structures, reinforcing groove while maintaining harmonic clarity. Cultural themes are approached with respect, rehearsed with discipline, and performed with emotional truth.

With Daniel Faria

In collaboration with Daniel Faria, Briki’s bass engages music shaped by Afro-descendant identity and contemporary Black Brazilian expression, while drawing on New Soul, New York, and British Hip Hop influences.

Their partnership reflects structural clarity aligned with rhythmic matrices that echo samba, funk, diasporic grooves, and global hip-hop sensibilities. Briki reinforces harmonic architecture while dialoguing with traditions rooted in resistance, celebration, and cultural continuity.

The collaboration is marked by maturity and mutual trust — technical precision aligned with ancestral consciousness and modern rhythmic vocabulary, shaping music that honors lineage while speaking to the present and the global scene.

With Ciro Belluci

Ciro Belluci is a multi-talented musician, composer, and interpreter whose work blends deep musical literacy with expressive performance, reimagining Brazilian classics and original compositions.

His collaboration with Briki focuses on structural interplay and creative composition, grounded in Afro-diasporic heritage and contemporary expression. Belluci has also participated in projects with rising artists, including Vanessa Moreno, demonstrating his commitment to fostering new voices and mentoring emerging talent.

With Cruvinel

Briki performed with Cruvinel in Groove do Cruv projects and a João Bosco tribute at Minas Tênis Clube, contributing bass lines that anchor groove and support melodic interplay.

Brazilian Jazz Scene Gigs

Briki’s work with contemporary Brazilian jazz ensembles highlights his versatility, improvisational skill, and engagement with Afro-Brazilian rhythmic traditions.

He has collaborated with the Dudu Amendoeira Trio, Lucas Demello Trio, Davi Desmolins Trio, Lucas Godoy Trio, and Carol Serdeira, performing complex compositions and exploring collective improvisation.

Within these trios and ensembles, Briki’s bass provides both harmonic foundation and rhythmic propulsion, supporting melodic interplay while emphasizing Afro-descendant influences. Each ensemble allows him to balance structured composition with expressive freedom, resulting in performances rich in cultural resonance, dialogue, and creative exploration.

Banda El Said

Banda El Said is a classical Arabic music group created by Khalil Briki, emphasizing the influence of Arabic music in the Western world. The band plays classical repertoire such as Om Kalthoum, Abdelhalim, and Abdelwaheb in a Brazilian context. Briki plays oud in this ensemble, connecting Arabic musical heritage to Brazilian and global audiences.

MUSIC EDUCATION & MENTORSHIP

Beyond performance, Khalil Briki is deeply committed to musical education and the preservation of cultural heritage. He has offered mentorship and courses in musicalisation, music perception, bass techniques, ensemble playing, and composition, impacting hundreds of students across Brazil and internationally.

Driven by a genuine love for knowledge distribution, Briki emphasizes listening, improvisation, structural understanding, and cultural literacy, fostering both technical development and artistic sensibility. His teaching balances discipline with creativity, encouraging students to explore personal expression while grounding their learning in solid musical fundamentals.

Briki’s mentorship also incorporates historical and cultural perspectives, highlighting the African and Afro-descendant roots of Brazilian and global music. Students gain insight into rhythmic matrices, harmonic architecture, and cross-cultural musical integration, bridging contemporary performance practices with ancestral knowledge.

Through workshops, private lessons, and ensemble coaching, Briki ensures that the richness of Afro-descendant and global musical heritage is passed on, celebrated, and sustained — cultivating new generations of musicians who are both technically skilled and culturally aware. His educational approach positions music not only as a craft but as a living cultural legacy capable of connecting communities, generations, and diverse traditions.
